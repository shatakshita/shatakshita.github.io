About Me

Some of the deepest questions humans have ever asked are about the universe itself — how it began, what it is made of, where it is going. Those questions are what drew me to astrophysics and cosmology, and they are what continue to shape everything I do. I am pursuing a B.Tech in Aerospace Engineering at SRMIST because I want to understand not just the cosmos we observe, but the systems we build to reach it.

Through my publication The Redshift Asterism, I write about cosmology, astrophysics, and the unresolved frontiers of modern science — bringing research-level ideas to a curious, general audience. Alongside that, I work as a Research Intern at ExoVista and contribute to citizen science initiatives through NASA and Zooniverse. Scientific computing is the thread that ties it all together: code is how I explore, model, and communicate ideas that otherwise live only in equations.

How I Think & Work

Research, writing, and computing all demand the same fundamental discipline: understand deeply before acting, and let the work speak for itself.

01

Break complex ideas into fundamental components

Whether analysing a cosmological model or debugging code, the first step is always decomposition — understanding what something actually is before deciding what to do with it.

02

Connect ideas across disciplines

Aerospace engineering, astrophysics, computation, and writing are not separate pursuits — they are different instruments playing the same inquiry. Cross-domain thinking produces the most interesting results.

03

Test through implementation and iteration

Theory alone is not enough. I validate ideas through code, writing, and practical application — iterating based on what actually works rather than what should work in theory.

04

Treat uncertainty as an invitation, not a barrier

The most interesting questions in science are unresolved ones. I am comfortable with incomplete knowledge and treat confusion as a signal to investigate further.

This way of thinking shapes everything — from how I approach a research question at ExoVista, to how I structure an article in The Redshift Asterism, to how I write code that makes a scientific idea tangible. The goal is always the same: understand deeply, communicate clearly, and contribute meaningfully.
