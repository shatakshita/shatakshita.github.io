About Me

Some of the deepest questions humans have ever asked are about the universe itself — how it began, what it is made of, where it is going. Those questions are what drew me to astrophysics and cosmology, and they continue to shape everything I do. I am pursuing a B.Tech in Aerospace Engineering at SRM Institute of Science and Technology (SRMIST) because I want to understand not just the cosmos we observe, but the systems we build to reach it.

Through my publication The Redshift Asterism, I write about cosmology, astrophysics, and the unresolved frontiers of modern science. Alongside that, I work as a Research Intern at ExoVista and contribute to citizen science initiatives through NASA and Zooniverse. Scientific computing is the thread that ties it all together — code is how I explore, model, and communicate ideas that otherwise live only in equations.

Long-term goal: To contribute to astrophysics and space exploration through research, aerospace engineering, and scientific computing — bridging the gap between the universe we study and the technology we build to explore it.
